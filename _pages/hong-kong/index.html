The ultimate city escape for urban explorers, Hong Kong mixes sleek, futurist towers with dynamic cultural delights at ground level. Discover cool cocktail hangouts, a trend-leading dining scene, and a unique nurturing of art and culture that plays itself out in groundbreaking cultural centres and a vast selection of on-trend galleries. Explore Hong Kong, discover its unique neighbourhoods, and immerse yourself in this breathtaking metropolis – only to escape it all at your next destination...

sections: - title: Old Town Central id: old-town-central subtitle: Modern skylines and modern art content: >

Amongst an awe-inspiring marvel of glistening towers and commuting suits hides a down-to-earth arts scene filled with locals...

Smart cocktail bars and a host of Michelin restaurants provide a network of stylish hangouts as the sun sets, but the trendy corner of Old Town Central is in SoHo. This artsy neighbourhood is the playground of Central’s bon vivants, where narrow streets shelter nascent galleries, smart boutiques, cool bars and renowned eateries. Art abounds on the street and in powerhouse cultural centres. Grab a coffee from an indie roastery, pick from acclaimed restaurants – this is a place to see and be seen.

Colonial buildings now host fine restaurants and narrow streets are filled with coveted boutiques – old meets new in Wan Chai.

One of the oldest parts of Hong Kong, heritage trails take you past icons like the Blue House, Pak Tai temple and colourful markets. Discover exclusive restaurants like Tom Aikens’ The Pawn, where colonial charms mix with a forward-thinking menu of delectable dishes. Peruse the fashionista boutiques of Star Street Precinct and let nostalgia beckon you to the colourful Tai Yuen Toy Market. Alight at a host of Dim Sum joints and as the sun sets, head over to Lockhart Road for down to earth revelry ‘til the early hours.

highlights: - id: wan-chai-heritage-trail - id: dim-sum - id: lockhart-road-parties - title: Sheung Wan id: sheung-wan subtitle: A hipster haven content: >

A transformed area, Sheung Wan has gone from sleepy suburb to hip, urban hangout.

As ever in Hong Kong, once cheaper rents saw an influx of artists and creatives, transforming the streets here into a warren of vintage shops, boutiques, indie roasteries and fashionable eateries. Hunt for treasure in the antique shops of Cat Street and Hollywood Street, before heading into PoHo for a healthy dose of local art and soy lattes. Galleries, indoors and on the street, showcase upcoming talent in the area, with designers flogging the latest fashions from small shops. Sheung Wan is a bona fide hipster hangout.

highlights: - id: cat-street-market - id: tai-ping-shan-street - id: yardbird - title: Sai Ying Pun id: sai-ying-pun subtitle: A foodie destination content: >

Known as a linchpin of the Hong Kong gastronomic scene, Sai Ying Pun is host to some of the most exciting new restaurants in the city.

As one of the oldest districts, this reinvention slots seamlessly into the locals’ feel of the area, where word-of-mouth buzz encourages bolder and bolder culinary treasures. You’re spoilt for choice come dinner time – expect everything from tapas to reinvented Cantonese feasts. Prop up in a local bar and ask around for the latest hot tip – Hong Kong encourages a dynamic and ever-changing food scene, so be sure to ask which latest venture is exciting the tastebuds of Hong Kong foodies.
