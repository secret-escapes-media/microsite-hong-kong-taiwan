sections: - title: Central & SoHo id: central-and-soho subtitle: Modern skylines and modern bars content: >

The shining epicentre of Hong Kong business, Central is an awe-inspiring marvel of glistening towers and fierce modernity.

It fills with workers throughout the day, who then spill into smart cocktail bars and a host of Michelin restaurants that provide a network of stylish hangouts as the sun sets. You’ll find luxury shopping to fill your days, framed by glass and steel monoliths. Take the Mid-Levels Escalator and alight at SoHo. This artsy neighbourhood is the playground of Central’s bon vivants, where narrow streets shelter nascent galleries, smart boutiques, cool bars and renowned eateries. Grab a coffee from an indie roastery, pick from acclaimed restaurants – this is a place to see and be seen.

Causeway Bay is for many about just one thing – shopping. From perfumed department stores to sleek malls, this area is geared for sheer retail therapy.

Head for the famous Sogo and Times Square Mall to start. If the seemingly endless throng of high-end brands in the malls becomes overwhelming, you’ll find unique purchases in the boutiques that line the Fashion Walk and the stalls along Jardine’s Crescent. To accommodate the crowds, cafes and eateries dot the area, particularly along the aptly named Food Street. If it all becomes a little much, head for Victoria Park, the largest in the city. For a truly tranquil spot, don’t miss the Tin Hau Temple gardens.

highlights: - id: boutique-shopping - id: food-street - id: times-square - title: Sheung Wan id: sheung-wan subtitle: A hipster haven content: >

A transformed area, Sheung Wan has gone from sleepy suburb to hip, urban hangout.

As ever in Hong Kong, once cheaper rents saw an influx of artists and creatives, transforming the streets here into a warren of vintage shops, boutiques, indie roasteries and fashionable eateries. Hunt for treasure in the antique shops of Cat Street and Hollywood Street, before heading into PoHo for a healthy dose of local art and soy lattes. Galleries, indoors and on the street, showcase upcoming talent in the area, with designers flogging the latest fashions from small shops. Sheung Wan is a bona fide hipster hangout.

highlights: - id: cat-street-market - id: tai-ping-shan-street - id: yardbird - title: Sai Ying Pun id: sai-ying-pun subtitle: A foodie destination content: >

Known as a linchpin of the Hong Kong gastronomic scene, Sai Ying Pun is host to some of the most exciting new restaurants in the city.

As one of the oldest districts, this reinvention slots seamlessly into the locals’ feel of the area, where word-of-mouth buzz encourages bolder and bolder culinary treasures. You’re spoilt for choice come dinner time – expect everything from tapas to reinvented Cantonese feasts. Prop up in a local bar and ask around for the latest hot tip – Hong Kong encourages a dynamic and ever-changing food scene, so be sure to ask which latest venture is exciting the tastebuds of Hong Kong foodies.
